Lego Project 2015

 

Project/Event

Lego Project

Organizer

ec100 Algorithms and computing

The Lego Project will span almost the entire duration of your course and will be divided into 3 modules. The first module aims to introduce you to lego robots and programming them. The second module aims to familiarize you with RobotC. In the last module you will be expected to solve a maze.

Important Milestones

Date

Description

Deliverables

March 12

Group Name/Team Submission

Submit Group Name on LMS with Team Members

March 16

Proposal Submission

2 Page proposal detailing the names of your group members, the resources you plan to use, how you plan to approach the problem, work distribution and timeline

March 18

Module 1

Issue kits on March 18.

March 25

Module 1 Video and Code Submission

Submit module 1. By the end of this module you should have created a lego robot and programmed it using the lego NXT Graphical language to follow a wall. You will submit a 5 minute video showing a demo of your robot, with each group member describing their contribution. You must also submit the project you created in the lego IDE.

March 25

Module 1 Report Submission

You will also submit a report detailing how you completed the module, what resources you used (please cite references), how much each group member contributed.

March 25

Module 1 Peer Assessment Submission/Kits Submission

These forms will be given to you and you must submit them. They count towards your final grade.

April 10

Module 2

Issue Kits

April 16-17

Module 2 Submission and Demo

Please make sure you have submitted your kits by 12 on Friday. You will have to show a demo of your robot following the wall and potting balls using RobotC. You can demo your robot anytime in the NERC Arena. You must also submit your code on the LMS.

April 18

Module 2 Peer Assessment

These forms will be given to you and you must submit them. They count towards your final grade.

April 28

Module 3

Issue Kits

May 11

Module 3 Submission and Demo

Please make sure you have submitted your kits right after your demo is over. You will have to show a demo of your robot solving a maze and potting balls in the correct boxes. You can demo your robot anytime in the NERC Arena. You must also submit your code on the LMS.

May 12

Module 3 Peer Assessment

These forms will be given to you and you must submit them. They count towards your final grade.
